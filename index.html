FAQ

Common questions

What exactly is briefing.fm?

A short news podcast, made just for you, waiting every morning. You choose the topics you're curious about; briefing.fm does the reading overnight and hands you only the headlines that matter — in one calm listen, usually five to ten minutes.

Where do the stories come from?

Overnight, AI reads across the live web on the topics you follow, keeps only what's genuinely worth knowing, and writes it into a tight radio script. Each story keeps its source, so you can always see — and open — where it came from.

Which podcast app do I listen in?

Whichever you already use — briefing.fm gives you a private podcast feed you add once to Apple Podcasts, Overcast, Pocket Casts, Spotify, or anything that takes an RSS link. New briefings then appear automatically each morning.

Can I add my own articles?

Two ways. Drop any link — an article, newsletter, or PDF — into your next morning briefing, summarised right alongside your topics. Or, when you can't wait, share one or more articles as a Special and get a briefing on just those, generated on the spot.

Is my account private?

Completely. Your topics and your feed are yours alone, secured by a secret link you can rotate at any time. There's no public profile and no social graph — just your briefing.

What does it cost?

While we're in beta, briefing.fm is free. After that it's $9/month, with a 7-day free trial — and no card needed to hear your very first briefing.
